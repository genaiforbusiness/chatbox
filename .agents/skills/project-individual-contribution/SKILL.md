# Individual Contribution Guide

This skill provides strategies and checklists to help you maximize your 10-point Individual Contribution grade by making your effort, proactivity, and teamwork visible in the repository.

## 1. Key Action Areas

### 1. Task Management & Completion
- **If it's not on GitHub, it didn't happen**: Document all research, design, and coding tasks inside GitHub Issues. Post updates and findings as comments.
- **Granular Issues**: Work with your team to break down large features into smaller tasks that can be finished in 1-2 days.
- **Link Commit Messages**: Always reference your issue numbers in your commit messages (e.g. `feat: Implement UI form. Closes #12`).

### 2. Communication & Proactivity
- **"Think out loud" on GitHub**: Post progress reports, questions, or blocks directly in your assigned issues.
- **Collaborate on Peer Issues**: Read your teammates' issues, offer tips, ask clarifying questions, and participate in discussion threads.
- **Create Proactive Issues**: If you discover a bug, identify a documentation gap, or see an opportunity for optimization, create a new issue for it.

### 3. Team Citizenship & Reliability (Peer Evaluated)
- **Be Reliable**: Complete tasks on time. Communicate *early* if a task is running late.
- **Constructive Reviewing**: When reviewing peer code or designs, focus on the work, keep feedback positive, and make suggestions actionable.
- **Un-glamorous Work**: Take your share of testing, writing documentation, and refactoring tasks.
