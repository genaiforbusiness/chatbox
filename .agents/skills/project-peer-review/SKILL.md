# Peer Review Guide

This skill helps you construct specific, constructive, and high-quality feedback when reviewing other teams' launch kits.

## 1. Review Framework

### Part 1: The Video Pitch (10 Points)
Evaluate the video based on:
- **Clarity of Purpose (4 pts)**: Was the business problem and the AI solution explained clearly and concisely?
- **Persuasiveness (4 pts)**: Was the pitch compelling? Did it make a strong case for user adoption or sponsor funding?
- **Professionalism (2 pts)**: Was the pacing, audio, and visual quality professional?

#### Video Written Questions
Provide specific, constructive responses:
1. *What was the single strongest element of their video pitch?* (Praise)
2. *What is one specific, actionable suggestion to make their video even more effective?* (Improvement)

### Part 2: The Quick-Start Guide (10 Points)
Evaluate `QUICK_START.md` based on:
- **Clarity for User (5 pts)**: Was the guide easy to follow? Did it avoid technical jargon? Was the language clear?
- **Effectiveness (5 pts)**: Do you feel confident that you could use the app's key features successfully after reading it?

#### Quick-Start Written Questions
Provide specific, constructive responses:
1. *What was the most helpful instruction or feature in their guide?* (Praise)
2. *What one part of the guide was the most confusing or could be simplified for a new user?* (Improvement)

## 2. Rubric for Completion
To earn full marks (5 points) on your reviews:
- [ ] Submit reviews for **both** assigned teams by the deadline.
- [ ] Answer **all** questions (both numeric scales and text boxes).
- [ ] Ensure feedback is **thoughtful, specific, and actionable** (one praise, one suggestion). Single-sentence generic phrases like "looks good" are insufficient.
